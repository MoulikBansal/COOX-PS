2Key Insights

🏙️ Bengaluru is Ground Zero

Bengaluru accounts for 143 refunded bookings — more than any other city. The majority originate from farmhouses and homes on the outskirts, especially along Devanahalli, Sarjapur, Yelahanka, and Kengeri belts.

🏡 Farmhouses Drive Refunds

Bookings to Farmhouses and event locations account for a disproportionate share of non-serviceable refunds. These are typically 15–40 km from city centres, well beyond delivery reach.

🔁 Repeat Offenders Exist

Multiple bookings from the same address make up a significant portion of losses. Some addresses have 3–8 repeat refunded bookings, indicating customers who don't yet know the area is non-serviceable.

📍 68 Natural Hotspots

68 geographic clusters were found using density-based analysis. 457 of 546 bookings (84%) fall into these clusters, proving this is a systematic, not random, problem.

7Strategic Recommendations
  1. Implement pin-code-level blocking — Add the 68 extracted pin codes to the COOX booking form validation. If a customer's delivery address matches a blocked pin code, show a friendly "We don't serve this area yet" message before payment.
  2. Add a real-time geo-fence check — Use the booking lat/lon at checkout to compute distance to the nearest operational kitchen. Reject bookings beyond the serviceable radius (e.g. 15 km) with an immediate UX notification.
  3. Block repeat-offender addresses directly — The top 15 addresses with 3+ bookings should be hard-blocked immediately by Address ID, preventing further revenue leakage from known problem locations.
  4. Educate customers proactively — For borderline areas, show a warning banner ("Delivery to this area may be delayed or unavailable") to set expectations before order placement.
  5. Monitor emerging problem areas — Rerun this clustering pipeline monthly on fresh refund data. New clusters will emerge as COOX expands, and the geo-blocking list should evolve accordingly.
  6. Explore targeted expansion — Clusters with the highest booking volume (e.g. Devanahalli belt in Bengaluru, OMR corridor in Chennai) may indicate unmet demand. Consider partnerships with kitchens in those areas to convert refunds into revenue.
8Expected Business Impact

💰 Reduced Revenue Leakage

Blocking 68 pin codes eliminates the bulk of non-serviceable bookings at the source, reducing refund processing costs and payment gateway fees.

😊 Better Customer Experience

Customers are informed before payment that their area is not covered, saving them the frustration of booking → waiting → cancellation → refund cycle.

⚡ Operational Efficiency

Customer support teams no longer need to manually process cancellations for known non-serviceable areas. Automated prevention replaces reactive handling.
